EvenGo Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-03

EvenGo is an event-planning prototype by Oliver Loeckler. This policy explains what data the app uses while helping users create events, invite participants, and request venue, vendor, and weather suggestions.

Data We Collect

  • Account data such as username, email address, account type, and hashed password.
  • Guest-session data such as an app-generated guest identifier and expiry date.
  • Event data such as title, description, date/time, city, venue, selected options, vendors, invite links, and participant names where enabled by the organiser.
  • Technical data needed to authenticate API requests and keep the service working.

Data We Do Not Collect

The Android app does not request device location, contacts, camera, microphone, SMS, call log, payment, or health permissions.

External Services

EvenGo uses public map and weather services through the backend. City names, event option categories, and search radius values may be sent to Nominatim, OpenStreetMap Overpass, and Open-Meteo to return venue, vendor, and weather suggestions. Account passwords and JWT tokens are not sent to these external lookup services.

How Data Is Used

Data is used to authenticate users, store events, show event dashboards, manage invite links, join participants to events, and provide planning suggestions. EvenGo does not sell user data.

Security

Release builds connect to the backend over HTTPS. Passwords are stored as hashes, and authenticated app requests use JWT bearer tokens.

Deletion

Users can delete their account from inside the app. Open the account menu and choose Delete account. Users who no longer have the app installed can use the instructions on the account deletion page.

Contact

For privacy questions, use the developer contact email shown on the EvenGo Google Play listing.
